Understanding the Data

How to Read This Data

The site shows the share of total wealth/income/emissions by any selected percentile range in the set country. For example, selecting 0–50% shows the wealth held by the bottom 50% of households, which is only a few percent. Selecting 90–100% corresponds to the top 10%. This includes all assets such as property, financial investments, business equity, minus debts.

Why Wealth Inequality Matters

High wealth inequality means a small number of households control a vast share of resources, limiting opportunity for the majority. This concentration affects social mobility, political influence, access to education, and economic stability. High inequality erodes trust, fuels social tension and can undermine democracy.

How You Can Help

Tackling wealth inequality is a complex issue, but there are steps you can take to help.
- Support and vote for parties that demand estate or wealth tax, typically left leaning
- Support small, local businesses
- Get informed about the issue and share it with others
- Join or form a union at your workplace
